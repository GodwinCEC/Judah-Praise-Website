About Us

The Christian Medical Fellowship - Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital (CMF-KATH) is a Christian interdenominational fellowship of KNUST medical students in their clinical years. The aim of our ministry is to have an impact on the medical profession and the entire nation as Christian medics.

For over 25 years, we have undertaken annual projects to support worthy causes at Komfo Anokye Teaching Hospital, Kumasi, making a lasting impact on healthcare delivery in Ghana.

The Stroke Crisis in Ghana

Understanding the urgent need for enhanced stroke care

Leading Cause of Death

Stroke is among the top five causes of adult admissions and deaths in Ghana, with significant impacts on individuals and families.

Strained Resources

The KATH Stroke Unit faces significant challenges due to limited resources, outdated equipment, and growing patient load.

Time-Critical Care

Early detection and timely treatment are crucial in reducing stroke-related deaths and disabilities, requiring modern equipment.

Project Aims & Objectives

Our comprehensive approach to enhancing stroke care

Medical Equipment Acquisition

Purchase patient cardiac monitors, mobile electrocardiogram machines, electronic hoist machines, and other essential equipment to expand the Stroke Unit's capacity and effectiveness.

Subsidizing Treatment Costs

Support underprivileged patients by covering treatment costs including medications and diagnostic tests, ensuring equitable access to quality stroke care.

Public Education & Advocacy

Empower communities through education about stroke prevention, healthy lifestyle promotion, and early detection through comprehensive outreach programs.
